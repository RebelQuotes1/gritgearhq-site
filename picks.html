Latest from the Blog

Grit in the Cold: My Les Stroud moment at 21 was when I first saw “Survivorman.” Les Stroud came on screen—calm, deliberate, alone in the wild—and something inside me lit up. It wasn’t inspiration. It was a challenge. I thought, “If he can do it, so can I.”

So, naturally, I packed the dumbest gear imaginable—gimmicky gadgets, canned food, and an unrated sleeping bag. I hiked alone into snowy woods. Below zero. I wore jeans. And steel-toe boots that nearly froze my toes off.

I survived by building a 7-foot fire and laying beside it, wrapped in a hoodie with my feet tucked in the sleeping bag. I used the “buddy system”—foot to foot for shared warmth. My boots? Charred by morning, but still wearable.

Lesson: Preparation matters. But it’s grit and instinct that save you. And shoutout to Les Stroud—the godfather of bushcraft—for planting that fire in me.
