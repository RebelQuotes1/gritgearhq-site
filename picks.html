🔥 My Les Stroud Moment

When I was 21, I watched *Survivorman* and thought: “If he can do it, so can I.” So I marched into the freezing woods alone, in steel-toe boots and jeans, dragging a pile of random gear. Romanticized misery turned into survival real quick.

I slept by a 7-foot longfire, boots off, feet wrapped in a hoodie using the old buddy-system—foot to foot for warmth. My boots got so close to the flames they were scorched by morning. I put out the coals, slipped on the crispy toes, and hiked out burned but proud.

Lesson: Never underestimate nature. Or your own grit. And shoutout to Les Stroud—the godfather of bushcraft—for lighting the fire inside me long before I lit that one in the woods.
