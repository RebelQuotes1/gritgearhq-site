The Night They Read Me My Last Rites

I shouldn’t be here. Years of hard drinking finally hit the wall. Stage 4 cirrhosis. A priest by my bed. Last rites. The kind of goodbye that leaves a mark you can feel for the rest of your life.

Walking out wasn’t triumph. It was hell with a pulse. Insomnia that stretched for days—sometimes a week. Pain everywhere. And an itch so deep it felt like being eaten from the inside. You can’t scratch it. You endure it.

Weed didn’t erase the pain. It changed it—from fire to a low electric hum. Still there, but survivable.

I wasn’t a smoker. Didn’t even have a plug. But something in me knew: try cannabis. That whisper saved my life. I could eat again. Sleep again. Be a husband, a father, a man again.
