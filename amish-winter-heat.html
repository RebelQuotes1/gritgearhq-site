Nights: How They Sleep Warm

Night strategy is where discipline pays off. The goal: coast until morning without turning the house into a meat locker.

Wood Discipline: What Most Folks Skip

Heat starts the year before you need it. That‚Äôs not romantic; it‚Äôs physics and planning. Here‚Äôs the short course.

Safety Truths (Non-Negotiable)

  • Ventilation: Stoves must be vented outside. A little fresh air feed is on purpose; starved fires are dirty and dangerous.
  • Chimney care: Burn hot enough to stay out of the creosote zone; brush mid-season; inspect after any weird smoke or draft behavior.
  • CO & smoke alarms: Battery-powered detectors belong in every off-grid or grid-down plan. Cheap insurance.
  • Clear zones: Nothing flammable leaning on the stovepipe. Spark screens for open doors. Fire extinguisher where you can grab it half-asleep.

Common Mistakes (and Easy Fixes)

  • Smoldering overnight burns: Low and smoky wastes wood and feeds creosote. Fix: hot, clean burns with dense wood; right-sized splits; proper draft.

  • Wet firewood: If it hisses, it‚Äôs stealing heat to boil itself. Fix: split smaller, bring in tomorrow‚Äôs wood to warm and dry, stack with airflow.
