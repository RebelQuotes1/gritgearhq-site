I Should Be Dead. Instead, I Sell Grit.

How I survived gangs, liver failure, and the slow death of the modern man.

I should be dead.

That’s not an exaggeration. It’s not a marketing hook. It’s a fact.

I’ve had a shotgun in my hands and murder in my heart. I’ve been tortured, jumped, left bleeding in the street. I’ve outlived gang wars, addiction, jail cells, and liver failure.

I’ve seen death. I’ve walked with him. And I told him, “Not yet.”

⛓️ Arkansas → Chicago → Warzone

I was born in the Delta, raised wild along the St. Francis River. I played in muddy water and fought in schoolyards. By 12, I was in too deep, Arkansas schools were ready to ship me off to juvie. So my dad sent me to Chicago.

I didn’t find peace there. I found a different battlefield.

I joined a gang because I didn’t see another option. It was survival. I took beatings. I gave worse. I earned rank, earned scars, earned enemies. I was kidnapped once. I was supposed to die. They let me live.

💀 The Slow Suicide of Addiction

I spent my 20s like I was invincible. Booze, chaos, violence, parties, lockups. I had a son at 19 but barely held on to the title of “father.”

Eventually, I woke up yellow, bloated, and half-dead. Stage 4 liver failure. No money. No will. Just a daughter too young to understand, and a wife who deserved better.

I prayed, not for a miracle, but for time. Time to be the man I never saw growing up. Time to be the father she could remember fondly and proudly.

🛠️ Rebuild from the Dirt

Against every odd, I survived. I quit drinking. I got strong. I started writing books. Started building a life.

I didn’t want therapy-speak or feel-good slogans. I wanted truth. Grit. Something raw enough to punch through the noise.

So I built Grit Gear HQ, not just a brand, but a warning.

If you’re soft, stay home.
But if you know life has teeth, welcome.

🔥 This Is the Grit Era

The world’s gotten fragile. Weak men cry online while real life falls apart.

I’m not here to motivate you. I’m here to remind you: pain is coming.

Whether it’s economic collapse, violence, or the demons in your own bloodstream, something’s going to try to take you out.

You better have something sharp in your hand and fire in your chest when it does.
