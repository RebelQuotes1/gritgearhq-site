PTSD: Survival Sabotage

Clinics describe PTSD in bullet points, but those points don’t bleed. Real life looks like this:

  • Sleep isn’t rest. It’s guard duty. You’re waking up screaming and scanning the dark at 3 a.m., waiting for a fight that never comes.
  • Anger misfires. Somebody cuts you off in traffic, and your brain thinks it’s a threat to your life and fight or flight kicks in.
  • Relationships take the hit. Not because you don’t care, but because hypervigilance burns you out. It can feel like there's not much left of yourself to give to anybody.
  • Appetite and energy crash. Trauma wrecks hunger, wrecks hormones, wrecks everything.

Now stack that on top of survivalism. We train to stay sharp, but PTSD hijacks that training and redlines the engine until it blows. That’s not sustainable. That’s sabotage. I need relief without losing readiness. That’s where cannabis steps in.

Why Cannabis Works

Your body’s got a built-in balancing system called the endocannabinoid system. Think of it as mission control for mood, sleep, pain, and stress. When trauma hijacks your system, cannabis compounds lock into that control board and reset the dials. No wonder one plant can handle so much:

  • Panic attacks: THC + calming terpenes quiet the noise without benzo apathy or hangover.
  • Sleep: Indicas pull you into real rest, not just sedation. Or are great for just sinking into your couch and watching a good movie.
  • Pain: THC + CBD together dull the volume without wrecking your clarity.
  • Appetite: Bud kicks hunger back on, which matters for healing and energy. Important for me because cirhossis wrecked my appetite.
  • Mood: The right strain can lift your head above water when depression drags you under.
One plant. Five jobs. That’s survival math.

Bud vs. Booze

Booze is the classic self-medication tool. I know, I lived it. A bottle quiets the noise for a few hours, then turns your life into ashes. Liver failure, rage, bad decisions, broken families—alcohol numbs but never heals.

Weed is different. With cannabis, you can ease your nerves, laugh with your crew, sleep like a human, and still wake up ready. No blackouts. No “what did I say last night?” It’s relief without chaos.

Bud vs. Big Pharma

Doctors love to prescribe, but the pharmacy lineup often makes life worse:

  • Benzos: Calm fast but hook you faster. Bud calms without chains.
  • Opioids: Kill pain but steal your life. Cannabis takes the edge off without turning you into a ghost.
  • SSRIs: Maybe help, maybe don’t; side effects can hollow you out. The right flower can lift mood in minutes.
  • Sleeping pills: Knock you out but rob deep sleep. Bud lets you wake up human.

One plant can do the work of half the cabinet. In a collapse, you won’t refill scripts anyway. Cannabis is the only medicine you can grow, cure, and store yourself.

Field Protocols (Run It Like Gear)

Sleep Loadout

Indica flower or a 10-25mg edible about 45 minutes before bed. Blackout room, cool air, phone in a drawer. Sleep like a mammal, not a lab rat.

Panic Loadout

Two deep breaths, 1–2 pulls on a vape. Hydrate. Step outside. Let your nervous system reset. Faster than a benzo, safer than a shot of whiskey.

Pain Loadout

Balanced THC:CBD strain. Small hits plus a topical cream on the joint or scar. Enough to move, enough to train, without checking out.

Resilience Loadout

Daily walk in the sun. Protein for breakfast. Cannabis microdoses when stress spikes. The plant is the assist—discipline is the foundation.
