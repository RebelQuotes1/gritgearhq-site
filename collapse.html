5 Things I Stopped Buying Once I Realized Western Society Is Doomed

Once you stop pretending things will “go back to normal,” your priorities shift. You start living like the system could fail tomorrow, because it might. I didn’t plan on becoming a survivalist. I just opened my eyes. And once I did, a lot of the things I used to spend money on started to feel... stupid.

Before I got serious about preparedness, my money leaked out of my wallet like a slashed tire. I’d buy whatever was convenient, trendy, or comforting in the moment. But survival doesn’t care about your cravings or your clutter. It demands discipline. It demands practicality. Here's five things I quit buying and what I replaced them with.

1. Bottled Water

Used to be, I’d grab a plastic bottle from the gas station without thinking twice, or lug home cases of bottled water. But over time, that convenience adds up in cost, trash, and wasted opportunity. Now I keep a Brita 10-Cup Filter Pitcher on standby. Not only does it save money, but in a grid-down scenario, it gives me a reusable way to clean tap water for daily use without having to crack open my long-term stash.

2. Gallons of Water

Instead of constantly buying one-gallon jugs, I invested in a solid Coleman 5-Gallon Water Carrier. It's durable, refillable, light can't penetrate making it perfect for long term storage and it's made for real use. Whether I'm camping off-grid or storing emergency water in the back of my SUV, this beats buying cases of plastic bottles that bust and collapse when you need them most.

3. Fast Food and Junk Snacks

Look, quitting ain't easy. But I stopped blowing cash on fast food and snacks that rot your insides and leave you lethargic. Now, I stock high-energy ration bars that not only last for 20 years but actually fill you up and fuel your body. One day’s worth of nutrition in a pocket-sized block. Made for war, suitable for comfort eating.

4. Frozen Dinners and Expensive Meat

Used to load up on frozen burritos and overpriced meat trays. Then I discovered shelf-stable canned meat that lasts years and costs next to nothing. Chicken of the Sea tuna is a staple in my bug-out bags and cabinets now. Packed with protein, easy to carry, and requires no prep. Plus, it makes a killer tuna melt over an open flame.

5. Deli Chicken and Takeout Protein

When the power's out or the store shelves are bare, that hot deli chicken you’re used to doesn’t mean jack. Swanson canned chicken breast is what I keep in my pantry and use often in things like Tuna-Helper or added to rice and other foods for added clean protein, long shelf life, decent taste, and loaded with survival-grade nutrition. Stack a few in the pantry and you’ll be able to make them go a long way if you get creative and you can rest easy knowing you're not one blackout away from hunger.

Once I shifted my mindset from convenience to preparedness, I stopped wasting money on garbage and started stacking supplies that actually serve me. Survival isn’t about luxury or tacti-cool gear, it’s about being ready. And being ready feels a hell of a lot better than being broke and scared. If you're prepared you don't have to be scared.
