The Death Valley Germans: A Desert Mystery Turned Tragedy

In the sweltering July heat of 1996, a young German family of four vanished without a trace in Death Valley National Park. Egbert Rimkus, a 34-year-old engineer, was joined by his son Georg, Egbert’s girlfriend Cornelia Meyer, and her 4-year-old son Max. They were adventuring across the American Southwest—until they weren’t.

Their rented minivan was found abandoned near Anvil Canyon. Flat tires. An empty water jug. A cryptic entry in the guest log at a remote ranger station simply read: "We are going over the pass." No one ever heard from them again.

For years, search efforts turned up nothing. It was like they had evaporated into the Mojave heat. It wasn’t until 2009—over a decade later— that searchers found bones scattered miles from the vehicle. The family had tried to hike out. No maps. No signal. No water. Just the endless, sun-bleached silence of the desert.
