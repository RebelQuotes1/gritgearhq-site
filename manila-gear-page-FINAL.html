Trapped in a Manila Stairwell

By the time I landed in Manila, I had already dodged more than a few shady situations. I’d spent time on the southern islands, where poverty clung to the air like smog and some neighborhoods made Chicago look like Disneyland. I ended up at an illegal cockfight where the energy in the room was thick — money flying, men screaming, roosters armed with razors tearing each other apart. It felt like the Russian roulette scene in The Deer Hunter — unspoken tension, and everyone pretending things were normal.

I knew I had no business being there, but that’s how I traveled then — chasing stories, letting instinct guide me, trusting my gut more than any guidebook.
